Frequently Asked Questions

Which loan is suitable for me?

The most suitable loan depends on your specific needs, income stability, and credit profile. Personal loans work well for salaried individuals with regular income. Business loans are ideal for entrepreneurs needing working capital. MSME loans offer government-backed support for small businesses. We provide personalized guidance to help you choose the right option.

Do you guarantee loan approval?

No, we do not guarantee loan approval. We are a loan advisory service that provides guidance and connects you with appropriate lenders. Final approval decisions are made by the financial institutions based on their lending criteria, your credit history, and documentation.

Can I apply with a low CIBIL score?

Yes, we work with customers across different credit profiles. While a higher CIBIL score improves approval chances, we can guide you on improving your credit score and finding lenders who consider other factors like income stability and business performance.

Do you charge any fees for your services?

Our basic loan advisory services are free. We may charge nominal processing fees only if you choose premium services like document preparation assistance or priority processing. All fees are clearly communicated upfront with no hidden charges.

How long does the loan advisory process take?

Initial eligibility assessment takes 24-48 hours. The complete process from consultation to loan disbursement typically takes 7-15 days, depending on the loan type, documentation completeness, and lender processing time. We keep you updated throughout the process.

What documents are required for loan application?

Common documents include identity proof (Aadhaar/PAN), address proof, income proof (salary slips/bank statements), and loan-specific documents. For business loans, additional documents like GST returns, business registration, and financial statements may be needed. We provide a complete checklist during consultation.

Do you work with all banks and NBFCs?

We have partnerships with multiple banks, NBFCs, and financial institutions across India. This allows us to offer you competitive options and help find the best fit for your requirements. We continuously expand our network to provide more choices.

Is my personal information safe with you?

Yes, we take data privacy seriously. Your information is encrypted, stored securely, and used only for loan advisory purposes. We comply with data protection regulations and never share your details with unauthorized parties without your consent.

Can I get guidance for multiple loan types?

Absolutely. Many customers benefit from guidance on multiple loan products. We can help you understand different options and choose the most suitable combination based on your financial goals and eligibility.

What if my loan application gets rejected?

If an application is rejected, we analyze the reasons and provide guidance on improving your profile. This may include credit score improvement tips, alternative loan options, or suggestions for strengthening your application for future attempts.

What is the difference between secured and unsecured loans?

Secured loans require collateral (like property or vehicles) as security, which generally offers lower interest rates and higher loan amounts. Unsecured loans don't require collateral but may have higher rates and lower amounts. The choice depends on what assets you can offer and your risk preferences. Learn more about secured vs unsecured loans.

Does applying for multiple loans affect my CIBIL score?

Multiple loan inquiries within a short period can temporarily impact your CIBIL score as they show up as credit inquiries. However, this effect is usually minor and short-lived. We recommend applying through a single advisor who can present your application to multiple lenders simultaneously to minimize inquiry impact.

How does income type affect loan eligibility?

Different income types (salaried, self-employed, business income) have varying documentation and verification requirements. Salaried income is often easier to verify, while self-employed applicants may need to provide additional financial documents. Lenders assess stability, consistency, and quantum of income for each type. Read our detailed guide on income types.

Are online loan advisors safe?

Reputable online loan advisors who are registered and follow RBI guidelines are generally safe. Always verify the advisor's credentials, check for proper licensing, and ensure they have clear privacy policies. Look for advisors who don't guarantee approvals and provide transparent fee structures. We maintain strict data security standards and comply with all regulatory requirements.

What should I check before signing a loan agreement?

Review the interest rate, processing fees, prepayment charges, and hidden costs. Verify the loan amount, tenure, and EMI calculations. Check for any cross-selling of insurance or other products. Ensure all terms match what was discussed. If anything is unclear, seek clarification before signing. Reading the fine print carefully can prevent future disputes.

When should I consider adding a co-applicant?

A co-applicant can help when your individual income or credit profile isn't sufficient for the desired loan amount. This is common for home loans, education loans, or when applying for higher amounts. The co-applicant's income and credit history are considered alongside yours, potentially improving approval chances and loan terms.

What is a loan moratorium and when is it useful?

A loan moratorium is a temporary pause in EMI payments, usually granted during financial difficulties. It's not automatic and depends on lender policies and your repayment history. Some loans offer built-in moratorium periods (like education loans). This can provide breathing room but may affect your credit score and increase total interest paid.

Is balance transfer always beneficial?

Balance transfer can be beneficial if you get a lower interest rate, but consider all costs including transfer fees, processing charges, and any changes in tenure. Calculate the total savings over the loan period. Sometimes staying with your current lender might be better if they offer better service or flexible terms. We can help compare options for your specific situation.
